NUEVA ECIJA PHPF CHAPTER

Pre Membership and Orientation Seminar

June 14, 2014

A pre-membership, value formation and orientation seminar was conducted last June 14, 2014 at exactly 9:00AM at San Antonio, Nueva Ecija. It was attended to by nineteen (19) participants who were pre-selected from the list of the DSWD.


Among other things, the following were discussed:

  1. The mission of PHPF as poverty alleviation, is to help the poor thru livelihood program, which will enable the family earn a regular income, become self-sufficient, and eventually “graduate” from PHPF.

  2. The adopted families were chosen from among the list of poor families of the DSWD. PHPF will only help those families who are willing, able and capable of undertaking a livelihood project.

  3. To be de-listed from the adopted family, the “DON’Ts “ were taken up:

    1. Non-cooperation with any of the programs of the NE PHPF;

    2. Drugs or alcohol abuse

  4. The adopted families were encouraged to attend Sunday mass obligation, and medical check-up, at least once a month.

  5. Filipino values, like honesty (katapatan), and trustworthiness (mapagkakatiwalaan) were inculcated in the minds of the group as values to be strengthened and nurtured, as these are the only wealth a poor individual has.

  6. Other benefits an adopted family can gain includes, but not limited to health program; scholarship to deserving children; supply of medicines, clothing, and things for personal use coming from the PHPF USA.

  7. A Memorandum of Agreement to be entered into by and between the NE PHPF Chapter and the Adopted Family (copy attached) was deliberated upon, and taken into consideration .

    1. Since the family has working experience in tambo making industry, it was unanimously agreed upon that the livelihood project would be tambo making;

    2. The Ph10,000.00 worth cost of raw materials for tambo making can be perfected into a finished product in a span of two (2) weeks;

    3. Marketing of finished products shall either be made locally on retail or wholesale basis. Most finished products shall be transported to Manila, on wholesale and consignment basis. The total amount of Ph10,000.00 shall be paid in full or installment, depending on the mode of payment of the buyer, for a period not to exceed three (3) months. Payment shall be made directly to the NE PHPF Chapter Treasurer RAYMUNDA M. CUANAN.

    4. The group came up with a time frame for the first project cycle, to wit:

      Delivery of raw materials/start of the project

      - June 14

      First monitoring schedule

      - June 23

      All products finished

      - June 30

      Last day of payment

      - Sept. 30

    5. Raw materials for tambo making industry shall be supplied by Mr. JUANITO BONDOC of San Antonio, Nueva Ecija, who offers the lowest price in town.

Each participant was required to fill out a personal information form


To formalize the election and makes it effective, an oath of office was initiated.


(A pose with the elected officers of the Adopted Family and the NE PHPF Chapter officers PERLITA FULGENCIO - Director, MARIANE ANGELICA CASTILLO - Asst. Director, LOURDES TAN FELIZ - Secretary and RAYMUNDA CUANAN - Treasurer)

Everyone was happy with the result of the orientation/seminar, and each kept their fingers crossed for the success of the project in general, and the upliftment of the lives of the adopted families in particular.
